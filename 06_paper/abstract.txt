Despite sustained investment in information and communication technology (ICT) in education, evidence on how ICT-related factors relate to student achievement remains mixed, particularly in middle-income contexts. This study examines ICT-related, contextual, and behavioural predictors of reading, mathematics, and science achievement in Thailand using PISA 2022 data. Random forest models were used as an exploratory predictive-screening tool to identify salient predictors while accommodating high-dimensional, non-linear, and interaction-rich data. To support interpretation, predictors were organised using the PISA questionnaire and ICT frameworks and an adapted Multi-Level Framework of Technology Acceptance and Use (MLFTAU). The analysis distinguishes global, in-school, out-of-school, and framework-specific patterns of predictive salience. Findings indicate that ICT-related predictors are best interpreted as part of broader configurations involving learner dispositions, socio-economic resources, school capacity, and learning environments. In-school patterns were more closely aligned with pedagogical and instructional conditions, whereas out-of-school patterns were more strongly associated with access, self-regulation, learner engagement, and home-resource conditions. Cross-framework synthesis identified recurring predictors, including curiosity, emotional control, ICT access quality, ICT self-efficacy, purposeful online practices, and socio-economic resources, alongside predictors whose salience varied by context or analytical framing. The study contributes a cross-framework predictive-screening approach for interpreting ICT-related achievement patterns in high-dimensional educational assessment data. The findings inform system-level interpretation of ICT-related predictive patterns rather than causal or population-level effect claims.
