Despite sustained investment in information and communication technology (ICT) in education, evidence on how ICT-related factors relate to student learning outcomes remains mixed, particularly in middle-income contexts. This study examines ICT-related, contextual, and behavioural predictors of achievement in reading, mathematics, and science in Thailand using data from the Programme for International Student Assessment (PISA) 2022. Random forest models are used as a predictive, non-parametric screening approach to identify salient predictors while accommodating non-linear relationships and interactions. Models are estimated separately across plausible values and aggregated to improve robustness. To support interpretation, predictors are organised across two complementary frameworks: the PISA Questionnaire and ICT Frameworks, and an adapted Multi-Level Framework of Technology Acceptance and Use (MLFTAU). The findings show that ICT-related predictors do not operate as isolated drivers of achievement but are embedded within broader systems shaped by behavioural engagement, socio-economic conditions, and learning environments. Clear differences emerge between in-school and out-of-school contexts, with pedagogical processes more salient in school settings and access- and self-regulation-related factors more prominent outside the classroom. Cross-framework synthesis further identifies both convergent predictors and framework-sensitive interpretations, underscoring the importance of analytical framing in understanding ICT–achievement relationships. Methodologically, the study shows how machine learning can be integrated into theory-driven educational research; substantively, it offers a system-level perspective on ICT in education. The findings are intended to inform interpretation of ICT investments rather than establish causal effects.
